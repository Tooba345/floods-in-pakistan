Building Resilience

How to avoid floods in Pakistan

No single measure can "stop" floods in Pakistan, but a combination of structural, nature-based and social solutions can significantly reduce disaster losses and protect vulnerable communities.

Structural & Nature-Based Solutions

Engineering plus ecosystems.

Stronger embankments & drainage

Upgrading river embankments, spurs, barrages and urban drainage systems can reduce the frequency of damaging floods. Design must take into account future climate-driven extremes, not just past records.

Retention basins & floodplains

Allowing certain zones to temporarily store floodwater – through retention basins, wetlands and protected floodplains – can lower peak flows and reduce pressure on embankments and cities downstream.

Ecosystem restoration

Reforestation in catchment areas, mangrove restoration along the coast and protection of wetlands can slow runoff, stabilise riverbanks and provide natural buffers against flooding.

Climate-resilient housing

Using raised plinths, flood-resistant materials, improved foundations and safe site selection for housing reduces the chance that families will lose everything in the next flood.

Information, Preparedness & Governance

Turning warnings into action.

Early warning systems

Modern weather forecasts, river gauge networks, flood modelling and mobile-phone alerts give communities more time to evacuate, protect livestock and secure essential documents.

Community-based disaster risk reduction

Training local disaster committees, organising evacuation drills, mapping safe routes and shelters, and involving women and youth in planning all improve local response capacity.

Land-use planning & building codes

Enforcing zoning regulations that restrict construction in high-risk areas, and applying resilient building codes, can prevent new risk from being built into cities and villages.

Social protection & insurance

Cash transfer programmes, crop and livestock insurance, and emergency social protection schemes help families absorb shocks and recover faster without falling into long-term poverty.

Good practice: Many successful pilots in Pakistan combine early warning, evacuation planning, resilient housing and livelihood support at the village level. Scaling these efforts is a key challenge.
