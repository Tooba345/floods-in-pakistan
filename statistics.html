Economic Losses

Billions of dollars in damage and lost income.

Cost of 2022 floods

Estimated $30–40 billion losses

Joint assessments by the Government of Pakistan and international partners estimate that the 2022 floods caused tens of billions of dollars in damage to housing, agriculture, transport, health, and other sectors.

Most affected sectors

Housing, agriculture & transport

Housing suffered some of the highest direct damage, followed by agriculture (crops, livestock, fisheries) and transport and communications infrastructure, including roads and bridges.

Recovery needs

Rehabilitation & reconstruction

Recovery needs include rebuilding climate-resilient housing, restoring livelihoods, rehabilitating irrigation systems, and upgrading roads and bridges to withstand future floods.

Trends & Future Risk

From rare disasters to more frequent extremes.

Climate change & extremes

Scientific studies show that intense multi-day rainfall events in southern Pakistan are likely to increase under high-emission scenarios, making "mega-floods" more common in the future.

AI-based forecasts

Recent research using artificial intelligence suggests Pakistan could face alternating episodes of super floods and severe droughts on a recurring basis, highlighting the need for long-term climate adaptation.

Urban flood risk

Rapid urbanisation without adequate drainage and planning increases the risk of urban flooding in cities like Karachi, Lahore and Peshawar, even during moderate rainfall events.
