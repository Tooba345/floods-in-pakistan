Policy & Planning

Government Flood Management Initiatives

Pakistan's federal and provincial governments have created agencies, plans and programmes to manage flood risk, from early warnings to reconstruction and climate-resilient development.

National Plans & Frameworks

Guiding preparedness and response.

National Disaster Management plans

Pakistan has developed national disaster management plans and policies that set out roles and responsibilities for federal, provincial and district authorities in preparing for and responding to floods and other hazards.

National Flood & Monsoon Contingency plans

Each year, the NDMA coordinates a National Monsoon Contingency Plan with provinces, focusing on forecasted rainfall, vulnerable districts, resource needs and standard operating procedures for evacuation and relief.

Urban flood management guidelines

Updated technical guidelines and studies on urban flooding provide recommendations on drainage, land use, early warning, and coordination between municipal and disaster management authorities.

Early Warning & Preparedness Initiatives

From forecasts to community action.

Strengthening early warning systems

The government is expanding weather and river-gauge networks and improving real-time data sharing between the Meteorological Department, NDMA, PDMAs and local administrations to issue more accurate and timely warnings.

Inclusive disaster risk management

Recent initiatives emphasise inclusive early warning systems, community engagement and guidelines that address the needs of women, children and persons with disabilities during evacuations and relief.

Preparedness drills & training

NDMA, PDMAs and NGOs frequently organise disaster preparedness training and simulation exercises with local authorities, volunteers and first responders.

Reconstruction & Climate-Resilient Recovery

"Build back better" after disasters.

Housing reconstruction in Sindh and other provinces

Large-scale housing reconstruction programmes aim to help families rebuild safer homes using climate-resilient designs, with financial and technical support from federal and provincial governments and international partners.

Resilient infrastructure projects

Road, bridge, irrigation and embankment rehabilitation projects increasingly incorporate climate-resilient standards to better withstand future floods and extreme weather.

Integrating climate change & DRR

Recent national and provincial policies highlight the need to integrate disaster risk reduction and climate adaptation across sectors such as agriculture, water, health and urban development.

Challenge: Effective implementation requires adequate funding, strong institutions, coordination between agencies, and participation by local communities who are most affected by floods.
